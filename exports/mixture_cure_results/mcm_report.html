Population Rationale

The Mixture Cure Model partitions the patient population into two latent groups: a cured fraction π(x) who will never experience recurrence, and a susceptible fraction 1−π(x) whose time to recurrence follows a Weibull distribution (shape=1.484, scale=139.88 y). The logistic incidence model identifies which covariates drive the probability of being in the susceptible (uncured) group.

Interpretation

Unlike the PTCM (which models the number of latent cancer cells), the MCM treats cure as a binary population split. Covariates with OR > 1 increase the probability that a patient belongs to the susceptible (uncured) group. The Weibull latency component governs when events occur among the uncured.
