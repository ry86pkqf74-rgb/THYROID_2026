# Suggested Limitations Paragraph

This study linked prospectively collected ACS-NSQIP thyroidectomy data
(2010–2023) to an institutional thyroid cancer research lakehouse using
deterministic matching on hospital medical record numbers, with
independent verification via sex, age, date of birth, and surgery date
concordance. Of 1,813 thyroid-CPT NSQIP cases, 1,803 (99.4%) were
successfully linked to 1,728 unique patients in the master cohort. Ten
cases (0.6%) could not be matched: three due to confirmed medical record
number collisions (different patients sharing the same identifier) and
seven because the patients were not present in any institutional source
file, likely reflecting care at affiliated sites not captured in the
primary data extract. The NSQIP thyroidectomy-specific module fields
(postoperative hypocalcemia, calcium/vitamin D replacement, and RLN
injury) were available for approximately 66–70% of matched cases, as
these data elements were introduced to the NSQIP thyroidectomy module
after 2015 and are not collected for all CPT codes. Consequently,
analyses of thyroidectomy-specific outcomes are restricted to the subset
with non-missing module data. General NSQIP fields (readmission, length
of stay, ASA classification, comorbidities, and preoperative laboratory
values) were available for all 1,803 matched cases. Patients with
multiple thyroidectomy surgeries captured in NSQIP (n=75, 4.3%) were
analyzed using their index (chronologically first) procedure for
patient-level summary statistics. As a single-institution study, these
results may not generalize to other practice settings. NSQIP 30-day
follow-up may underestimate complications with delayed presentation,
particularly permanent hypoparathyroidism and late recurrent laryngeal
nerve dysfunction.
