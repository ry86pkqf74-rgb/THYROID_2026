# Suggested Limitations Paragraph

This study linked prospectively collected ACS-NSQIP thyroidectomy data
(2010–2024) to an institutional thyroid cancer research lakehouse using
deterministic matching on hospital medical record numbers, with
independent verification via sex, age, date of birth, and surgery date
concordance across four institutional source files. Of 1,281 NSQIP Case
Details records, 1,275 (99.5%) were successfully linked to 1,261 unique
patients in the master cohort. Six cases (0.5%) could not be matched:
one due to a confirmed medical record number collision (different
patient sharing the same identifier), one due to an unresolvable MRN
absent from all institutional source files, and four because the
patients were not present in any institutional data extract, likely
reflecting 2024 cases not yet incorporated into the research data
warehouse or care at affiliated sites. The NSQIP thyroidectomy-specific
module fields (postoperative hypocalcemia, calcium/vitamin D
replacement, and RLN injury) were available for approximately 74–75% of
matched cases (939–945 of 1,261 patients), as these data elements were
introduced to the NSQIP thyroidectomy module in later years and are not
collected for all CPT codes. Consequently, analyses of
thyroidectomy-specific outcomes are restricted to the subset with
non-missing module data. General NSQIP fields (readmission, length of
stay, ASA classification, comorbidities, and preoperative laboratory
values) were available for all 1,275 matched cases. Patients with
multiple thyroidectomy surgeries captured in NSQIP (n=14, 1.1%) were
analyzed using their index (chronologically first) procedure for
patient-level summary statistics. As a single-institution study, these
results may not generalize to other practice settings. NSQIP 30-day
follow-up may underestimate complications with delayed presentation,
particularly permanent hypoparathyroidism and late recurrent laryngeal
nerve dysfunction.
